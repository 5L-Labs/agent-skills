# Additional Theme Families

This reference extends the canonical unified digest taxonomy beyond the original 7 top-level themes. It is intended for cross-platform digest skills and weekly/monthly rollups that need broader coverage than the original AI-centric set.

## 1. Geopolitics & World Affairs

**What it covers:** International conflict, diplomacy, U.S. politics, government power, trade, sanctions, and major world-affairs coverage.

**Sub-themes**

| Sub-Theme | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| Middle East | Iran, Israel, Gaza, the Levant, and broader regional conflict/diplomacy |
| China / Taiwan | China policy, Taiwan, Hong Kong, export controls, and regional strategy |
| U.S. Politics | White House, Congress, elections, courts, agencies, and federal policy |
| Europe / Russia / Ukraine | Europe-wide politics, NATO, Russia, Ukraine, and security posture |
| Trade / Sanctions | Tariffs, sanctions, export controls, and economic statecraft |

**Examples**
- Iran/Israel crisis analysis
- U.S. court blocks a policy move on H-1B visa fees
- Trump / Fed / administration coverage
- China export-control and Taiwan conflict analysis

**Overlap guidance:** If the article is about AI *inside* geopolitics, keep the primary signal. A story on AI export controls belongs here if the main point is state power; it belongs under AI & ML Research only if the main point is the model or method itself.

## 2. Career & Leadership

**What it covers:** Hiring, recruiting, founder journeys, executive moves, management, leadership development, identity transitions, and workplace org design.

**Sub-themes**

| Sub-Theme | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| Hiring & Recruiting | Candidate sourcing, interview loops, recruiting strategy, job markets |
| Executive Moves | CEO/VP changes, promotions, org charts, leadership reshuffles |
| Founder / Operator | Founder stories, operator lessons, startup leadership, company building |
| Identity / Growth | Career transitions, identity shifts, personal growth, coaching |
| Product Leadership | Product management, product orgs, roadmap leadership, cross-functional execution |

**Examples**
- senior leader dealing with repeated reorganizations
- founder identity reset after a shutdown
- hiring manager / talent machine stories
- product builder playbooks and leadership interviews

**Overlap guidance:** If the content is mostly about the product itself, use Product & Growth. If it is mostly about the human/career side of the work, use Career & Leadership.

## 3. Product & Growth

**What it covers:** Product strategy, launch stories, consumer apps, feature rollouts, growth loops, retention, monetization, and UX changes.

**Sub-themes**

| Sub-Theme | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| Consumer Apps | Consumer product launches, app behavior, social/app mechanics |
| Launches & Features | New product releases, features, UX additions, app updates |
| Monetization | Pricing, subscriptions, revenue optimization, business model changes |
| Growth Strategy | Acquisition, retention, virality, distribution, funnel strategy |
| Platform UX | Interfaces, onboarding, workflows, user experience, product design |

**Examples**
- app overhaul ahead of IPO
- product teams shipping new workflows or AI features
- consumer app virality and growth case studies
- monetization or pricing changes in software products

**Overlap guidance:** If the story is about the business of the product, keep it under Industry & Business. If it is about how the product feels/works and how users adopt it, use Product & Growth.

## 4. Science & Health

**What it covers:** Health, medicine, biology, genetics, brain research, climate, space, and adjacent science reporting that is not primarily business or AI.

**Sub-themes**

| Sub-Theme | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| Medical Research | Clinical research, diagnoses, treatments, hospitals, patient care |
| Biology / Genetics | Biology, genetics, genomics, proteins, wet-lab science |
| Brain / Cognition | Brain science, cognition, neuroscience, mental performance |
| Climate / Environment | Climate, ecology, weather, sustainability, environmental science |
| Space | Space systems, astronomy, rockets, orbital research |

**Examples**
- health risk studies
- paper on brain activity and writing
- quantum crypto or space-related science articles
- biology / genomics breakthroughs

**Overlap guidance:** If the story is about the business or product side of health tech, use Industry & Business or Product & Growth. If it is a science finding, keep it here.

## 5. Community & Events

**What it covers:** Meetups, conferences, webinars, bootcamps, community roundups, talks, office hours, and event-driven programming.

**Sub-themes**

| Sub-Theme | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| Conferences | Formal conferences, summits, symposiums, flagship events |
| Meetups | Community meetups, local gatherings, recurring meetups |
| Webinars | Online sessions, livestreams, virtual workshops |
| Hackathons | Hackathons, bootcamps, coding events, demo days |
| Talks / Workshops | Talks, panels, office hours, roundtables, workshops |

**Examples**
- conference recap or event roundup
- summit announcement
- webinar invitation or live session reminder
- bootcamp / hackathon announcement

**Overlap guidance:** If an email is mostly a promo for attending, use this theme or the operational event labels. If the email is a substantive essay that happens to mention an event, keep the essay theme and ignore the event mention.

## Decision Flow for New Themes

When deciding whether to introduce one of these as a top-level theme:

1. Use the primary signal first.
2. Prefer existing top-level themes if the new bucket would only fragment a small number of stories.
3. Promote a new top-level theme when a recurring sender/news source clearly and repeatedly maps to it.
4. Add sub-themes only when they reduce ambiguity in a measurable way across multiple sources.

## Usage

Use these themes in the same way as the original canonical 7:
- theme bucketing in cross-platform digest jobs
- weekly/monthly rollups
- preview site grouping
- future classifier backporting
